Notice of Privacy Practices

THIS NOTICE DESCRIBES HOW YOUR HEALTH INFORMATION MAY BE USED AND DISCLOSED AND HOW YOU CAN ACCESS THIS INFORMATION. PLEASE REVIEW IT CAREFULLY.

This notice of Privacy Practices describes how our practice may use and share your health information with others to carry out treatment, payment, or health care operations and for other purposes that are permitted or required by law. It also describes your rights to view and amend your Protected Health Information ("PHI"). PHI information is information about you and the services you have received. This includes information such as your name, address, date of birth, diagnosis, treatment, or other information that could identify you and your past, present, or future physical or mental health or treatment you receive.

Uses and Disclosures of Your Health Information

Your PHI may be used and shared by your physician, our office staff, and others outside of our office who are involved in your care and treatment for the purpose of providing health care services to you, paying claims for health care provided to you, and any other use permitted or required by law.

Treatment

We will use and share your PHI to provide, coordinate, or manage your health care and any related services. This includes coordinating your health care with a third party (e.g., sending PHI to a specialist as part of a referral).

Payment

Your PHI will be used, as necessary, to receive payments for claims related to the services provided to you. For example, obtaining approval for a hospital stay may require sharing your PHI with your health plan. We may also disclose your PHI to billing companies, claims processors, and other third parties who handle insurance claims.

Company Operations

We may use or disclose your PHI to support our health care operations, such as:

  • Quality assessment and employee review activities
  • Medical student training, licensing, and audits
  • Marketing and fundraising activities
  • Business operations with attorneys, accountants, and consultants
  • Registration sheets at the front desk and calling patients by name in the waiting room

Appointment Reminders

We may contact you to remind you of medical appointments via phone or email.

Treatment Alternatives

We may inform you of possible treatment options and health-related benefits and services that may interest you.

Other Situations Without Your Authorization

  • As Required by Law: Disclosure under federal, state, or local law.
  • Public Health Activities: Reporting to public health authorities.
  • Abuse and Neglect: Reporting to public officials authorized to receive such reports.
  • Health Oversight Activities: Disclosures to oversight organizations.
  • Legal Proceedings: Disclosures in judicial or administrative proceedings.
  • Law Enforcement: Disclosures to law enforcement in certain situations.
  • Coroner and Organ Donation: Disclosures for identifying deceased persons, organ donation, or funeral arrangements.
  • Research: Disclosures approved by an institutional review board.
  • To Prevent Serious Harm: Disclosures to prevent a serious threat to public or personal safety.
  • Government Functions: Disclosures for military, national security, and inmate-related purposes.
  • Workers' Compensation: Disclosures as required by law for work-related injuries or illnesses.

Other Permitted and Required Uses and Disclosures

Other uses and disclosures not described in this notice will only be made with your authorization or opportunity to object unless required by law. This includes uses of psychotherapeutic notes, marketing purposes, and the sale of your PHI. You may cancel any authorization in writing to our Privacy Officer.

Your Rights

  • Access: You can inspect and request copies of your PHI. Requests must be in writing and may include a reasonable fee.
  • Restrictions: You may request limits on PHI use or disclosure.
  • Confidential Communications: Requests for confidential contact methods must be submitted in writing.
  • Paper Copy: You may obtain a paper copy of this notice at any time.
  • Amendments: You may request corrections to your PHI; denials may be reviewed and you may submit a statement of disagreement.
  • Accounting of Disclosures: You may request information about certain disclosures of your PHI.
  • Notification of Breach: You will be notified of any breaches of unsecured PHI.
  • Complaints: You may file a complaint with our office or the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services.

We reserve the right to change the terms of this notice. Revised notices will be posted in our office and on our website.

We are required by law to protect your information, provide this notice, follow the practices described herein, and notify you of any breaches. For questions or complaints, contact us at (972) 876-4005 or info@absoluteeyecare.org.
