Notice of Privacy Practices

THIS NOTICE DESCRIBES HOW YOUR HEALTH INFORMATION MAY BE USED AND DISCLOSED AND HOW YOU CAN ACCESS THIS INFORMATION. PLEASE REVIEW IT CAREFULLY.

This Notice of Privacy Practices describes how our practice may use and share your health information to carry out treatment, payment, or health care operations and for other purposes that are permitted or required by law. It also describes your rights to view and amend your Protected Health Information ("PHI").

PHI is information about you and the services you have received. This includes your name, address, date of birth, diagnosis, treatment, or other information that could identify you and your past, present, or future physical or mental health care.

Uses and Disclosures of Your Health Information

Your PHI may be used and disclosed by your physician, our office staff, and others outside our office who are involved in your care for treatment, payment, health care operations, or as permitted or required by law.

Treatment: We may use and disclose your PHI to provide, coordinate, or manage your health care, including referrals to third parties.

Payment: We may use and disclose your PHI to obtain payment for services provided to you, including submitting claims to insurance providers, Medicare, or Medicaid.

Health Care Operations: We may use or disclose your PHI to support business operations such as quality assessment, employee review, audits, licensing, marketing, fundraising, and compliance with applicable laws.

We may also use sign-in sheets, call you by name in the waiting room, or disclose PHI to attorneys, accountants, or consultants as necessary.

Appointment Reminders: We may contact you by phone or email to remind you of appointments.

Treatment Alternatives: We may inform you of treatment options or health-related services that may be of interest.

Other Permitted and Required Disclosures

We may disclose your PHI without authorization as required by law, for public health activities, abuse or neglect reporting, health oversight, legal proceedings, law enforcement, coroners, organ donation, research, preventing serious harm, government functions, and workersâ€™ compensation.

Other Uses and Disclosures

Uses and disclosures not described in this notice will be made only with your written authorization, unless otherwise permitted or required by law. You may revoke an authorization at any time in writing.

Your Rights

You have the right to inspect and obtain a copy of your PHI, request restrictions, request confidential communications, request amendments, receive an accounting of disclosures, obtain a paper copy of this notice, and receive notice of breaches of unsecured PHI.

You also have the right to file a complaint with our office or with the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services if you believe your privacy rights have been violated. Filing a complaint will not affect your care.

Changes to This Notice

We reserve the right to change the terms of this notice. Revised notices will be posted in our office and on our website.
